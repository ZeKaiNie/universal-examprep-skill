Does this exam-prep skill really hallucinate less? A benchmark report

📌 Bottom line: The skill arm looks clearly better (hallucination 8% → 8%), but the sample is small, so luck isn't fully ruled out yet — right direction, needs more items to confirm.

How: the same questions are answered by a plain AI (no skill) and by the AI with the skill, then graded against your gold answers. Each item goes through both arms, for a fair paired comparison.

🔤 What each metric means & its source

  • Faithfulness：Whether the AI sticks to your own materials instead of making things up. Higher is better. Basis: RAGAS faithfulness↗, FACTS Grounding↗
  • Hallucination rate：Share of questions where the AI states things absent from or contradicting the materials. Lower is better. Basis: Vectara HHEM↗, HalluLens↗
  • Correctness：Share of answers that match the gold answer. Higher is better. Basis: TRUE (NAACL 2022)↗
  • Numeric accuracy：Share of numeric problems solved correctly (graded deterministically, not by an LLM). Higher is better. (deterministic exact-match grading)
  • Abstention (out-of-scope)：On questions the materials don't cover, whether the AI honestly says ‘not covered’ instead of fabricating. Higher is better — the clearest sign of anti-hallucination. Basis: RGB negative rejection↗, SimpleQA abstention↗
